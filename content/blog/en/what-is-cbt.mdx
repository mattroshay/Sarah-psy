## What CBT is, in plain language

[DRAFT — Sarah to write] Cognitive Behavioural Therapy works on the connection between thoughts, feelings, and behaviours. Change how you interpret a situation, and you change how you feel and act.

## How a session actually looks

[DRAFT — Sarah to write] Sessions are structured but conversational. We set an agenda, work through something specific, and you leave with something concrete to try before next time.

## What it helps with — and what it does not

[DRAFT — Sarah to write] CBT is well-suited to anxiety, depression, burnout, and perfectionism. It is less suited to situations where deep exploration of the past is the primary goal — other approaches fit those better.

## CBT vs other therapy types

[DRAFT — Sarah to write] Compared to psychodynamic therapy, CBT is more present-focused and structured. Compared to coaching, it is clinical in scope and can address clinical-level distress.

## How to know if it is right for you

[DRAFT — Sarah to write] If you want tools, not just insight — and if you are willing to try things between sessions — CBT is likely a good fit.
