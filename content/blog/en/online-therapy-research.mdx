## The most common hesitations about online therapy

[DRAFT — Sarah to write] "Can it really be as good as in-person?" Yes, and for some people and situations, it is better. Here are the hesitations I hear most often — and what the evidence says.

## What research says about online CBT effectiveness

[DRAFT — Sarah to write] Multiple meta-analyses show online CBT to be as effective as in-person CBT for anxiety and depression. The therapeutic relationship — the part that matters most — transfers well to a screen.

## What makes online therapy work, and what does not

[DRAFT — Sarah to write] Consistency, a private space at home, and a reliable connection matter more than platform. What does not work is treating sessions as something to fit between tasks.

## Who online therapy is especially suitable for

[DRAFT — Sarah to write] Expats, parents with childcare constraints, professionals with unpredictable schedules, and anyone who has moved repeatedly — for all of these, the flexibility of online therapy is a genuine advantage.

## How my sessions work in practice

[DRAFT — Sarah to write] Sessions are 50 minutes, on a secure video platform, weekly or bi-weekly. A free 20-minute discovery call comes first.
