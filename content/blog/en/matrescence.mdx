## What matrescence is — and why it is not postnatal depression

[DRAFT — Sarah to write] Matrescence is the psychological, neurological, and social transformation of becoming a mother. It is not a disorder — it is a developmental phase, like adolescence, that every mother goes through.

## The identity, relationship, and career shifts it brings

[DRAFT — Sarah to write] Your relationship with yourself, your partner, your work, your body, and your sense of purpose can all shift simultaneously. The lack of language for this experience makes it harder, not easier.

## Why it often goes unspoken

[DRAFT — Sarah to write] There is enormous pressure to frame new motherhood as purely joyful. The ambivalence, grief, and disorientation that are part of the experience rarely get named.

## How CBT and parenting support can help

[DRAFT — Sarah to write] Parenting-specific support combines practical tools for managing anxiety with space to process the identity shift itself. Both are necessary.
